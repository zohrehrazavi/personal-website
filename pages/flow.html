My approach to project delivery and workflow methodology

My approach to project delivery blends structured planning with adaptability. I use agile principles, but I'm not tied to one methodology, instead, I tailor my process to each project's needs, team dynamics, and goals.

1

Discovery & Requirements

  • Understand the business challenge and define the core problem.
  • Engage stakeholders to capture needs, expectations, and constraints.
  • Outline success metrics and project scope.
2

Planning & Architecture

  • Break down the project into milestones and deliverables.
  • Select tools, tech stack, and frameworks best suited for the project.
  • Map dependencies, risks, and potential bottlenecks.
3

Design & Prototyping

  • Collaborate with designers to translate requirements into wireframes and UI mockups.
  • Review early prototypes with stakeholders for feedback and alignment.
4

Development & Iteration

  • Coordinate with developers to ensure feature implementation matches specs.
  • Use agile sprints for incremental delivery and regular demos.
  • Maintain clear, continuous communication between tech and business teams.
5

Testing & Quality Assurance

  • Define testing strategies: unit, integration, and UAT.
  • Prioritize fixing critical issues before release.
  • Conduct user testing for final polish.
6

Launch & Handover

  • Oversee deployment to production.
  • Provide documentation, training, and support handover.
  • Track performance and user feedback in the first release phase.
7

Continuous Improvement

  • Collect data and feedback post-launch.
  • Identify opportunities for optimization and iterate on the product.
  • Keep the roadmap updated with future enhancements.
