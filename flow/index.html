Flow

How I run projects from discovery to delivery, ensuring quality outcomes and stakeholder alignment.

1

Discover

I start by deeply understanding the problem space through stakeholder interviews, user research, and data analysis. This phase focuses on uncovering the root causes, user pain points, and business objectives that will guide the entire project. I create comprehensive discovery documents that serve as the foundation for all subsequent decisions.

2

Define

With insights gathered, I work with stakeholders to clearly define success criteria, scope boundaries, and key deliverables. This includes creating user stories, acceptance criteria, and technical requirements. I establish clear communication channels and ensure all parties have a shared understanding of what success looks like before moving forward.

3

Prioritize

I use frameworks like MoSCoW (Must-have, Should-have, Could-have, Won't-have) and value vs. effort mapping to create a prioritized roadmap. This phase involves tough decisions about what to build first, what can wait, and what might not be needed at all. I ensure the team focuses on high-impact, low-effort items that deliver immediate value.

4

Deliver

Execution happens in short, iterative cycles using agile methodologies. I maintain close collaboration with development teams, conduct regular stakeholder reviews, and ensure quality gates are met at each milestone. Throughout delivery, I monitor progress, identify risks early, and adjust plans based on feedback and changing requirements.

5

Iterate

After delivery, I gather user feedback, analyze performance metrics, and identify opportunities for improvement. This continuous improvement cycle ensures that solutions evolve based on real-world usage and changing business needs. I document lessons learned and apply them to future projects, creating a culture of continuous learning.
