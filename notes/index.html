Notes

Short insights and observations from my work in product management and project delivery.

The Power of "No" in Product Management

Learning to say no to good ideas is often more valuable than saying yes to great ones. Every feature request that gets built means something else doesn't. Focus on impact, not just innovation.

User Research ≠ User Feedback

User research uncovers needs and behaviors. User feedback often just tells you what users think they want. The former drives strategy, the latter validates solutions. Don't confuse them.

Agile Isn't About Speed

Agile methodologies aren't about delivering faster—they're about learning faster. Each sprint should reduce uncertainty, not just produce code. If you're not learning, you're not being agile.

Metrics That Matter

Vanity metrics make you feel good. Leading indicators help you predict outcomes. Lagging indicators confirm what happened. Build your dashboard around the middle category.

Stakeholder Management 101

Your job isn't to make everyone happy—it's to make the right decisions and bring people along. Transparency about trade-offs builds trust, even when the news isn't what they want to hear.

Technical Debt vs. Product Debt

Technical debt slows down developers. Product debt slows down users. Both matter, but product debt is often invisible to engineering teams. Make it visible in your planning.

Documentation as a Competitive Advantage

Good documentation doesn't just help your team—it helps your users, your stakeholders, and your future self. Invest in it early, maintain it religiously, and watch productivity soar.

Meeting Effectiveness

If you can't articulate what decision needs to be made or what information needs to be shared, don't have the meeting. Send an email instead. Your team will thank you.

Failure as a Learning Tool

Projects fail for many reasons, but the biggest failure is not learning from them. Conduct retrospectives, document lessons learned, and create processes that prevent the same mistakes.

Team Velocity vs. Team Health

A team can deliver fast while burning out, or slower while building sustainable practices. The second approach wins in the long run. Monitor both metrics and prioritize accordingly.
